💡 Grammar help:

Present Simple

Great for habits, routines, facts, and preferences.

Affirmative: Subject + verb (+ s/es he/she/it)
I live in the city. / She loves cooking.
Negative: Subject + don't / doesn't + verb
They don't work on Sundays. / He doesn't eat meat.
Question: Do / Does + subject + verb?
Do you like music? / Does she travel a lot?

Past Simple

Use it to talk about completed actions or experiences in the past.

Affirmative: Subject + verb-ed / irregular form
I visited Paris last summer. / We went to the beach.
Negative: Subject + didn't + verb (base)
She didn't call me yesterday. / I didn't sleep well.
Question: Did + subject + verb (base)?
Did you enjoy the trip? / Did they arrive on time?

Past Continuous

Use it for actions in progress at a specific past moment, or as a background to another past event.

Affirmative: Subject + was/were + verb-ing
I was reading when you called. / They were cooking dinner.
Negative: Subject + wasn't / weren't + verb-ing
He wasn't listening to the teacher.
Question: Was/Were + subject + verb-ing?
Were you sleeping at 8 pm?

Present Perfect

For experiences, changes, or actions that connect the past with now. Common with: already, yet, ever, never, just, for, since.

Affirmative: Subject + have / has + past participle
I have visited Japan. / She has just finished the book.
Negative: Subject + haven't / hasn't + past participle
I haven't tried sushi yet. / He hasn't called back.
Question: Have / Has + subject + past participle?
Have you ever been to London?

Conditionals 0 & 1

Zero: general truths. First: real or possible situations in the future.

Zero (general truth): If + present, present
If you heat water to 100°C, it boils.
First (real future): If + present, will + verb
If it rains tomorrow, I will stay home.
Negative: If + don't / doesn't / won't...
If you don't study, you won't pass the exam.

Talking About the Future

English has several ways to talk about the future — each one has a different feel.

Will → spontaneous decisions, predictions, offers
I will help you. / It will be a great trip!
❌ I won't forget to call you.
Going to → plans already decided, predictions based on evidence
We are going to travel next month. / It is going to rain — look at those clouds!
❌ She is not going to study abroad.
Present Continuous → fixed arrangements with others
I am meeting Maria at 6 pm. / We are flying to Rome on Friday.
❌ They aren't coming to the party.
