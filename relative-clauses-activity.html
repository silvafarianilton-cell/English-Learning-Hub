Quick Reference

who

for people
The man who lives next door is a doctor.

which

for things / animals
The book which I bought is fascinating.

whose

possession (people & things)
The student whose essay I read was brilliant.

where

for places
The café where we met closed down.

when

for times
I remember the day when we first met.

that

informal — replaces who/which in defining clauses
The film that won the Oscar was incredible.

Defining vs. Non-defining

Defining: essential info — no commas.
The woman who called you is waiting.

Non-defining: extra info — use commas.
My sister, who lives in Berlin, is visiting next week.
